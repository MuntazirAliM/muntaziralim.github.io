About

Building things that
actually work

I'm an MSc Data Science student at King's College London with a BSc in Software Engineering. My work sits at the intersection of rigorous data science and practical engineering — I care about models that perform, pipelines that run in production, and outputs that non-technical stakeholders can actually use.

I've built financial risk platforms grounded in SEC filings, clinical readmission models with SHAP explainability, and real-time data pipelines from raw hardware inputs. Every project is built to be deployed, not just demonstrated.

Outside the technical work, I'm genuinely curious about the intersections between data and decisions — whether that's in financial markets, healthcare systems, or behaviour change communications.
